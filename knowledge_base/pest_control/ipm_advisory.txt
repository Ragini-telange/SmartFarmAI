PEST & DISEASE CONTROL ADVISORY
================================
SmartFarm AI Knowledge Base – Integrated Pest Management (IPM) Guide

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
SECTION 1: GENERAL IPM PRINCIPLES
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Integrated Pest Management (IPM) is an eco-sensitive approach that combines:
1. Cultural controls (crop rotation, resistant varieties, spacing)
2. Mechanical/physical controls (traps, barriers, manual removal)
3. Biological controls (natural enemies, Bt, NPV)
4. Chemical controls (pesticides as last resort)

ALWAYS follow the IPM pyramid: Cultural → Mechanical → Biological → Chemical.

SECTION 2: COMMON INSECT PESTS – ALL CROPS
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

PEST: American Bollworm / Pod Borer (Helicoverpa armigera)
-----------------------------------------------------------------
Crops affected: Cotton, Tomato, Chickpea, Maize, Sorghum, Sunflower
Symptoms: Circular holes in bolls/pods/fruits; caterpillar inside fruit
IPM:
  Cultural: Destroy crop residue; deep ploughing post-harvest
  Mechanical: Pheromone traps 5/ha (Helilure); light traps 1/ha
  Biological: SpNPV @ 250 LE/ha; Bt (Bacillus thuringiensis) @ 1 kg/ha
  Chemical: Emamectin 5 SG @ 0.4 g/l; Spinosad 45 SC @ 0.3 ml/l
  Note: Rotate insecticides to avoid resistance; do NOT use pyrethroids alone.

PEST: Whitefly (Bemisia tabaci)
-----------------------------------------------------------------
Crops affected: Cotton, Tomato, Okra, Brinjal, Chilli
Symptoms: Yellow stippling on leaves; sticky honeydew; sooty mould; virus vector (TLCV, CLCuV)
IPM:
  Cultural: Remove weed hosts; avoid continuous cotton; use reflective mulch
  Mechanical: Yellow sticky traps @ 10/ha; screen nurseries
  Biological: Encarsia formosa parasitoid; Neem oil 5% spray
  Chemical: Spiromesifen 22.9 SC @ 1 ml/l; Diafenthiuron 50 WP @ 1 g/l
  Note: Imidacloprid 70 WG as seed treatment delays early infestation.

PEST: Aphids (Various species)
-----------------------------------------------------------------
Crops affected: Wheat, Mustard, Chilli, Tomato, Brinjal, Pulses
Symptoms: Curling leaves; sticky honeydew; yellowing; transmit mosaic viruses
IPM:
  Cultural: Avoid excessive N; plant border crops of coriander/mustard for natural enemies
  Biological: Chrysoperla larvae @ 10,000/ha; Syrphid fly conservation
  Chemical: Dimethoate 30 EC @ 1.5 ml/l; Thiamethoxam 25 WG @ 0.5 g/l

PEST: Brown Plant Hopper / BPH (Nilaparvata lugens)
-----------------------------------------------------------------
Crops affected: Rice (paddy)
Symptoms: "Hopperburn" – circular drying patches from base; honeydew + sooty mould
IPM:
  Cultural: Avoid excess N; wider spacing; drain field periodically
  Biological: Mirid bugs, spiders as natural predators
  Chemical: Imidacloprid 200 SL @ 0.25 ml/l; Buprofezin 25 SC @ 1.5 ml/l
  Note: Never spray pyrethroids (resurgence trigger).

PEST: Termites (Odontotermes spp.)
-----------------------------------------------------------------
Crops affected: Sugarcane, Wheat, Groundnut, Cotton
Symptoms: Hollow stems; wilting plants; underground tunnels
IPM:
  Cultural: Remove tree stumps; avoid raw FYM; flood irrigation kills termites
  Chemical: Chlorpyrifos 20 EC soil drench @ 2.5 L/ha; Fipronil 0.3G @ 33 kg/ha

PEST: Stem Borer (Chilo partellus, Sesamia inferens)
-----------------------------------------------------------------
Crops affected: Maize, Sorghum, Rice, Sugarcane
Symptoms: Dead heart in vegetative stage; white ear in reproductive stage
IPM:
  Mechanical: Light traps; egg mass collection
  Biological: Trichogramma chilonis egg parasitoid @ 1 lakh/ha × 3 releases
  Chemical: Cartap HC 4G @ 18 kg/ha; Chlorantraniliprole 18.5 SC @ 0.3 ml/l

SECTION 3: MAJOR CROP DISEASES
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

DISEASE: Blast (Pyricularia oryzae) – RICE
-----------------------------------------------------------------
Symptoms: Diamond-shaped lesions with grey centres on leaves; neck rot
Conditions: Cool (<25°C), humid nights; continuous rice cultivation
Management:
  Resistant varieties: CR-Dhan 801, Saket-4
  Seed treatment: Thiram 75 WS @ 4 g/kg
  Foliar: Tricyclazole 75 WP @ 0.6 g/l at boot-leaf stage; Isoprothiolane 40 EC @ 1.5 ml/l
  Avoid: High N; dense nursery; stagnant water

DISEASE: Late Blight (Phytophthora infestans) – POTATO/TOMATO
-----------------------------------------------------------------
Symptoms: Dark water-soaked lesions on leaves; white mould underside; tuber rot
Conditions: Humid (>80% RH), 10–20°C temperatures; epidemic in hills
Management:
  Preventive: Mancozeb 75 WP @ 2 g/l every 7 days when weather is conducive
  Curative: Metalaxyl + Mancozeb @ 2.5 g/l; Dimethomorph 50 WP @ 1 g/l
  Cultural: Certified seed; avoid overhead irrigation; destroy infected haulm

DISEASE: Powdery Mildew (Erysiphe/Sphaerotheca spp.)
-----------------------------------------------------------------
Crops: Cucurbits, Grapes, Wheat, Pea, Chilli
Symptoms: White powdery growth on upper leaf surface; stunted growth
Management:
  Cultural: Improve air circulation; reduce humidity
  Biological: Sulphur 80 WG dust @ 25 kg/ha
  Chemical: Hexaconazole 5 SC @ 2 ml/l; Myclobutanil 10 WP @ 1 g/l

DISEASE: Fusarium Wilt (Fusarium oxysporum) – MULTI-CROP
-----------------------------------------------------------------
Crops: Tomato, Cotton, Banana, Chickpea, Watermelon
Symptoms: One-sided yellowing then wilting; brown vascular discolouration
Management:
  Cultural: Crop rotation (4-year minimum); avoid furrow irrigation
  Biological: Trichoderma harzianum @ 4 kg/ha soil application
  Chemical: Carbendazim 50 WP seed treatment 2 g/kg; soil drench @ 1 g/l

DISEASE: Downy Mildew (various oomycetes)
-----------------------------------------------------------------
Crops: Cucumber, Onion, Grapes, Pearl Millet (Green Ear disease)
Symptoms: Yellow angular patches on upper leaf; grey-purple sporulation below
Management:
  Metalaxyl + Mancozeb @ 2.5 g/l; Copper oxychloride @ 3 g/l
  Crop rotation; destroy infected debris

SECTION 4: NEMATODE MANAGEMENT
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Root-Knot Nematode (Meloidogyne incognita):
- Crops: Tomato, Brinjal, Okra, Groundnut, Banana
- Symptoms: Galls/knots on roots; stunted yellowing plants
- Management:
    Cultural: Marigold (Tagetes erecta) intercrop or rotation – suppressive
    Biological: Paecilomyces lilacinus / Purpureocillium lilacinum @ 4 kg/ha
    Chemical: Carbofuran 3G @ 33 kg/ha; Ethoprophos 10G @ 15 kg/ha

SECTION 5: PESTICIDE SAFETY GUIDELINES
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

⚠️  SAFETY DISCLAIMER: Always follow label directions. Wear PPE (gloves, mask, goggles). 
    Observe pre-harvest intervals (PHI). Avoid spraying near water bodies. 
    Store chemicals in original containers away from children and food.
    Dispose empty containers safely. Report poisoning to nearest PHC immediately.

Banned Pesticides in India (as of 2023, selected list):
  Monocrotophos, Endosulfan, Chlordane, Aldrin, DDT, Methyl Parathion, 
  Carbofuran (proposed ban under review), Chlorpyrifos (proposed restrictions).
  
  Always check CIBRC (Central Insecticides Board & Registration Committee) list before use.

Pre-Harvest Intervals (PHI – days before harvest):
  Imidacloprid: 7 days | Chlorpyrifos: 15 days | Mancozeb: 7 days
  Tricyclazole: 42 days (rice) | Spinosad: 3 days | Emamectin: 5 days
