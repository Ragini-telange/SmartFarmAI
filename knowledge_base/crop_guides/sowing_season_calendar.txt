CROP SOWING SEASON CALENDAR – INDIA
=====================================

This document provides a comprehensive seasonal crop recommendation guide for Indian farmers based on season, region, and soil type.

=======================================================================
KHARIF SEASON (Monsoon crops – Sown: June–July; Harvested: Sept–Nov)
=======================================================================

CROP         | STATES                          | SOIL TYPE              | NOTES
-------------|----------------------------------|------------------------|----------------------------------
Rice/Paddy   | All India                        | Clay / Clay-loam       | Needs standing water
Maize        | Karnataka, MP, Rajasthan, UP     | Sandy loam, red soil   | Can be rainfed
Sorghum(Jowar)| Maharashtra, Karnataka, AP      | Black cotton, red soil | Drought tolerant
Pearl Millet | Rajasthan, Gujarat, Haryana      | Sandy soil             | Very drought tolerant
Groundnut    | Gujarat, AP, Rajasthan, TN       | Sandy loam, red soil   | Needs well-drained soil
Soybean      | MP, Maharashtra, Rajasthan       | Medium black soil      | N-fixing legume
Cotton       | Gujarat, Maharashtra, Telangana  | Deep black (regur)     | 180-day season
Sugarcane    | UP, Maharashtra, TN, Karnataka   | Deep loamy             | Plant Feb–April; ratoon Kharif
Turmeric     | AP, Odisha, Tamil Nadu, WB       | Loam, well-drained     | 8–9 months duration
Ginger       | Kerala, Meghalaya, Karnataka     | Loam, forest soil      | Shade tolerant variety available
Arhar/Tur    | Maharashtra, UP, Karnataka, MP   | Red/black              | Semi-perennial; N-fixer
Urad/Moong   | AP, Rajasthan, UP, MP            | Sandy loam             | Short-duration 65–75 days

=======================================================================
RABI SEASON (Winter crops – Sown: Oct–Dec; Harvested: Feb–Apr)
=======================================================================

CROP         | STATES                          | SOIL TYPE              | NOTES
-------------|----------------------------------|------------------------|----------------------------------
Wheat        | Punjab, Haryana, UP, MP, Raj     | Loam, clay-loam        | Irrigated preferred; 6 irrigations
Barley       | Rajasthan, UP, MP, Bihar         | Sandy loam, saline OK  | Drought tolerant; malt + feed
Gram/Chickpea| MP, Rajasthan, Maharashtra, AP   | Medium black, loam     | Rainfed possible; N-fixing
Lentil(Masur)| UP, Bihar, MP                   | Loam                   | 110–120 days
Mustard      | Rajasthan, UP, Haryana, MP       | Sandy loam             | Oilseed; 90–120 days
Sunflower    | Karnataka, AP, Maharashtra       | Black/red loam         | Also Kharif and Zaid possible
Potato       | UP, WB, Punjab, Haryana, Bihar   | Sandy loam, well-drain | 70–100 days; cool weather
Onion        | Maharashtra, Karnataka, MP, Raj  | Medium loam, well-dr.  | Rabi onion: Oct–Nov sowing
Coriander    | Rajasthan, MP, Gujarat           | Sandy loam             | 100–120 days

=======================================================================
INTERCROPPING SYSTEMS
=======================================================================

MAIN CROP  | INTERCROP         | BENEFIT
-----------|-------------------|-----------------------------------------------
Sugarcane  | Onion / Garlic    | Extra income; early canopy fills gap
Cotton     | Blackgram/Moong   | Short-duration filler; N-fixation
Maize      | Cowpea            | Shade tolerant legume; soil health
Arhar (Tur)| Sorghum           | Wind break; mutual benefit
Groundnut  | Castor            – Border cropping; pest management (castor as trap crop)
Wheat      | Mustard           | Not recommended (compete for light)

=======================================================================
NOTES FOR ADVISORS
=======================================================================
- Always confirm local agroclimatic zone before final recommendation.
- Check for recent pest/disease alerts from State Agriculture Department.
- Recommend certified seed from government-approved sources.
- Encourage soil health card recommendations for fertiliser dose.
