CROP CULTIVATION GUIDE – TOMATO (Solanum lycopersicum)
=======================================================

OVERVIEW
--------
Tomato is India's most widely grown vegetable. Major producing states: Andhra Pradesh, Madhya Pradesh, Karnataka, Gujarat, Odisha.

SUITABLE CONDITIONS
-------------------
- Season       : Kharif (June–Sept), Rabi (Oct–Jan), Zaid (Feb–May)
- Temperature  : 21–29 °C (optimum); fruit set fails above 35 °C or below 10 °C
- Rainfall     : 600–1,200 mm or drip-irrigated
- Soil         : Well-drained loamy soil, pH 6.0–7.0
- Altitude     : Up to 1,500 m

POPULAR VARIETIES & HYBRIDS
----------------------------
1. Pusa Ruby          – Determinate; disease tolerant; fresh market
2. Arka Vikas         – Indeterminate; good for processing
3. Arka Rakshak       – Resistant to Tomato Leaf Curl Virus, Early Blight; IIHR
4. NS-816 (hybrid)    – Long shelf life; popular in Nasik
5. Rupali             – Maharashtra; dual-purpose

NURSERY & TRANSPLANTING
-----------------------
- Seed rate : 150–200 g/ha
- Nursery   : Raised beds; thin sowing, 25–30 day old seedlings
- Spacing   : 75 cm × 60 cm (staked) or 120 cm × 45 cm (bush type)

NUTRIENT MANAGEMENT (per hectare)
----------------------------------
- N: 180 kg (split: 1/3 basal + 1/3 first fruit set + 1/3 second fruit set)
- P₂O₅: 100 kg (basal)
- K₂O: 150 kg (split like N)
- Calcium foliar spray reduces blossom-end rot.

IRRIGATION
----------
Drip irrigation highly recommended; reduce water stress at flowering/fruiting.
Critical stages: transplanting, flowering, fruit development.

PEST & DISEASE MANAGEMENT
--------------------------
Diseases:
1. Early Blight (Alternaria)      – Mancozeb 75 WP @ 2 g/l every 10 days.
2. Late Blight (Phytophthora)     – Metalaxyl + Mancozeb @ 2.5 g/l preventively.
3. Tomato Leaf Curl Virus (TLCV)  – Control whitefly vector with Imidacloprid 70 WG @ 0.3 g/l; use resistant varieties.
4. Fusarium Wilt                  – Soil drench with Carbendazim @ 1 g/l; crop rotation.

Pests:
1. Whitefly          – Yellow sticky traps + Neem oil 5 ml/l + Imidacloprid.
2. Fruit Borer       – Helicoverpa: Spinosad 45 SC @ 0.3 ml/l or Bt spray.
3. Mites (Red Spider) – Dicofol 18.5 EC @ 2 ml/l or Abamectin 1.9 EC.
4. Aphids            – Dimethoate 30 EC @ 1.5 ml/l; avoid broad-spectrum for beneficials.

POST-HARVEST
------------
Shelf life: 3–5 days at room temp; 2 weeks at 12–13 °C, 90–95% RH.
Grading: A (>75g), B (50–75g), C (<50g).
Average yield: 25–50 t/ha (hybrid, protected cultivation higher).

MARKET INFORMATION
------------------
Tomatoes are price-volatile; sell in APMC mandis or through FPOs/farmer markets.
Peak arrivals: Nov–Feb in North India; May–July in South India.
Key mandi markets: Lasalgaon (MH), Kolar (KA), Madanapalle (AP).
